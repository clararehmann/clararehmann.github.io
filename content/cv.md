## Research Experience

### PhD Student
> Advisor: Dr. Andrew Kern, Institute of Ecology & Evolution, University of Oregon — September 2022–present
Developing and applying geographically-informed population genetics methods with a focus on _Anopheles gambiae_. Projects include correcting for spatial bias in machine learning inference of location from genetic data, constructing open-source resources for population genetic simulation and analysis, and using novel space-based methods to identify positively-selected variants.

### Rotation Student
> Advisor: Dr. William Cresko, Institute of Ecology & Evolution, University of Oregon — March 2022–June 2022
Assessed the impact of varying cross-species scRNA-seq integration methods on analysis outcomes in order to improve identification of developing facial structures in Syngnathids.

### Rotation Student
> Advisor: Dr. Michael Harms, Institute of Molecular Biology, University of Oregon — September 2021–December 2021
Investigated population-level effects on possible evolutionary trajectories across a fitness landscape with high-order epistasis using individual-based simulation.

### Scientific Programmer
> Advisor: Dr. Andrew Kern, Institute of Ecology & Evolution, University of Oregon — June 2020–June 2021
Developed Python plotting tools for machine learning methods and assessed the impact of biased dispersal on machine learning inference of location from genetic data.

### Research Assistant
> Advisors: Dr. Matthew Barber, Dr. Kristin Kohler, Institute of Ecology & Evolution, University of Oregon — April 2020–June 2021
Used computational and experimental methods to investigate coevolutionary dynamics between primate lysozyme and bacterial lysozyme inhibitors.

### Undergraduate Researcher
> Advisor: Dr. Beth Lazazzera, University of California, Los Angeles — September 2017–June 2018
Experimental work investigating impacts of inaccurate translation caused by the loss of aminoacyl-tRNA synthetase editing on _E. coli_ fitness.
